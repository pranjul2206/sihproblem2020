    		Government of Andhra Pradesh APPCB looks forward to bringing in all polluting industries in the state to enhance the existing system more transparent and effective monitoring of emissions and effluents. APPCB envisages implementing Internet of Things (loT) technology for obtaining of authentic and reliable data directly from the analyzers installed at the industry rather than the data from the intermediary system.
    Problem Statement Details
    Description
    Andhra Pradesh Pollution Control Board (APPCB) is a statutory organization entrusted to implement Environmental Laws and control pollution in the state of Andhra Pradesh through regulations. APPCB is presently using cloud-based software to connect all the analyzers installed at the industries to obtain real time data. The data received is analyzed to monitor the exceedances against the prescribed standards for each industry. APPCB looks forward to bringing in all polluting industries in the state to enhance the existing system more transparent and effective monitoring of emissions and effluents. As an extensively modernizing state, Andhra Pradesh is developing its industrial sector vigorously. Industries being significant source of both emission and effluents, are required to be monitored stringent monitoring. APPCB uses monitoring tools namely Continuous Emission Monitoring System (CEMS) and Continuous Effluent Quality Monitoring System (CEQMS) to check release of polluting emission and effluent to the environment. CEMS and CEQMS comprises analytical components and are continuous real time tools, providing monitoring data every minute. Although there is a system of real time monitoring of polluting emissions and effluents from industries, APPCB is trying to develop a more efficient system that can provide authentic Continuous Emission Monitoring System (CEMS) and Continuous Effluent Quality Monitoring System (CEQMS). CEMS and CEQMS are calibrated in such a way that it collates data from different types of industries falling under different make and model as well as pollution category. Since each industrial sector under each pollution category may have set emission and effluent standards as prescribed by Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) or standardized by APPCB as per the local environmental conditions, ensuring reliability and consistency of the data received through CEMS and CEQMS are important. Other areas of improvement for implementation of CEMS/CEQMS are as follows: Ensuring quality assurance of equipment and suitable technology, most reliable method of installation of analyzers, effectual reliable operation, ensuring credible data, viable data transfer calibration and maintenance of the equipment etc. Additionally, enhancement of the existing online monitoring system is also required for development of an unswerving emission and effluent database for the state of Andhra Pradesh. Above mentioned are the need of the hour for Andhra Pradesh state as 13 town/cities in the state are already listed in the list of Non-Attainment cities w.r.t air pollution and five river stretches are categorized as polluted. It is crucial for APPCB as the key regulatory agency for pollution in the state. Current System using in detailed: Andhra Pradesh Pollution Control Board uses cloud-based software to connect all the analyzers installed at the industries to obtain real time data. The data received is analyzed to monitor the exceedances against the prescribed standards for each industry. APPCB looks forward to bringing in all polluting industry categories existing in the state to be adequately monitored to check pollution in the long run. Significant indicators taken into consideration under CEMS are the emission and effluent quality standard limits as prescribed by the Board based on the industry type, category, local conditions and the characteristics of the emission and effluents by the industry. APPCB envisages implementing Internet of Things (loT) technology for obtaining of authentic and reliable data directly from the analyzers installed at the industry rather than the data from the intermediary system.

    		Ministry of Rural Development Automatic Assessment of Pavement condition based on road photographs
    Problem Statement Details
    Description

An AI assisted module would be able to automatically assess the picture and identify common issues such as shoulder clearance, potholes, road furniture etc. Requirement is of a solution where there should be a provision to capture the chainage wise pavement condition index. Use of open source software and existing neural network is encouraged. Train a machine learning model, computer vision etc. which is able to identify common issues with pavement based on photograph(s) per road alone.     		Ministry of Rural Development Automation mechanism for image capturing, processing, standardization, evaluation, classification leading to better decision making results for various programs
    Problem Statement Details
    Description
    Images are captured as part of validation/proof under various programs for separate reasons. * Images of houses being constructed under PMAY-G, works/assets created under MGNREGA, PDFs (Placement documents, Offer letter, Salary Slips etc.) being uploaded under DDUGKY validating proof of employment, Pictures of Works like Road Construction/Upgradation, Drain Construction/Upgradation, Canal Irrigation Construction etc. under Rurban.PMGSY Program are captured. Pics are majorly being uploaded through Mobile App having timestamp & geo tagged data. Physical verification/review of images is done for each pic. Collectively, over 8 crore images have been collected in multiple DBs. It is required that an automated solution (including Deep learning, image processing algorithms, AI algorithms etc.) be developed which is able to automatically identify the type of image (preferred 3D capture), classify and segment, validate and categorize nature of image type, identify the object, define boundaries etc. highlight duplicates (Plagiarism) , invalid pics etc. A software may be developed which at time of photo capture is able to capture, analyse and classify pixels, read text (OCR, Google lens etc.) and help in defining type. Would be helpful if it is able to calculate length, breadth, width (depth) etc. also. A holistic solution (Automation) is required which can help address these gaps, simplify the process and provide better results. The solution may reference 3D image (GIF), image processing technologies, image analysis, optimization, segmentation, and restoration, algorithm, computer vision and image processing (CVIP) technologies etc. Would help if solution devised is light weight (size of photo capture) and using open source tools. Sample Data Required: No

    		Ministry of Rural Development Intelligent attendance solution for MGNREGA workers
    Problem Statement Details
    Description
    Under MGNREGA, wage payment is made to workers doing unskilled work and subsequent creation of assets. A muster roll is prepared (consisting of list of workers who are working on a particular asset/work ) and attendance is entered. Based on no of days attended/worked, the payment is released. Currently capturing of attendance is a manual process where a physical entry is made by the work supervisor and no proper attendance tracking is done. There is no checking or effective/efficient verification process involved. An intelligent attendance solution is desired to be developed (out of the box thinking will be appreciated ad not just limited to a Mobile App based solution) which is feasible, less complex in implementation, address all challenges and eliminates the gaps in current system promoting transparency and less corruption/fraudulent entries. The system developed/proposed should be fool proof, preferred automated with minimal manual intervention. Latest technological interventions (use of Advanced Analytics, BI, AI, Machine learning etc.) should be referenced for developing a state of the art solution Sample Data Required: No
